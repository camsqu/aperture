# Aperture Science — Company History

Aperture Science was founded in the 1940s by Cave Johnson. The company began as
**Aperture Fixtures**, a manufacturer of shower curtains, with its first major
contract supplying curtains to the United States military.

Over the following decades the company pivoted dramatically toward experimental
research, rebranding as Aperture Science Innovators. Key eras include:

- **1950s — Aperture Science Innovators:** Early experiments with manufacturing and
  materials science, including the famous (and largely useless) moon-rock healing gels.
- **1960s–1970s — The Enrichment Center:** Construction of the underground testing
  facility and the beginning of formal human (and occasionally less-than-voluntary)
  test subject programs.
- **1980s — Portal research:** Development of the Aperture Science Handheld Portal
  Device, enabling instantaneous spatial displacement.
- **Modern era — GLaDOS:** Deployment of the Genetic Lifeform and Disk Operating
  System (GLaDOS) to manage the facility and oversee testing.

Aperture's motto, coined by Cave Johnson, is: **"We do what we must, because we can."**
